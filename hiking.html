What to pack

  • Layered clothing (weather changes fast)
  • Water & snacks (nuts, fruit, chocolate)
  • Map / offline GPS and a power bank
  • Rain shell and warm hat
  • First-aid mini kit & blister care
  • Headlamp (even in summer, just in case)

Safety & etiquette

  • Tell someone your plan & ETA
  • Stay on marked trails; respect signs
  • Leave no trace — pack out all waste
  • Yield to uphill hikers; control dogs
  • Check weather and daylight before you go

Spring

Snow melts on higher paths; waterfalls are strongest. Trails can be muddy — waterproof boots help.

Summer

Longest days and warm lakes. Popular routes can be busy — start early for quieter trails.

Autumn

Golden forests and crisp air. Pack a warm layer; daylight hours drop quickly.
